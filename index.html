Shelters & Help

Find current shelter and aid information

  • Call the Joint Information Center at 719‑583‑4640 for Pueblo and Custer County assistance information.
  • Ask whether a shelter accepts pets, mobility equipment, or large animals before traveling.
  • Call ahead to confirm hours, capacity, and accessibility.

When you need help leaving

  • Tell dispatch or the Joint Information Center if transportation, medication, oxygen, or mobility support is needed.
  • Bring identification, medications, assistive devices, and pet supplies if directed to leave.
  • Do not rely on a prior meeting or shelter location without current confirmation.

Be Ready — Especially for Older Adults & Seniors

Go-bag (keep by the door)

  • Medications — 7+ days + written list
  • Glasses, hearing aids, mobility aids, CPAP
  • ID, insurance/Medicare cards, key documents
  • Phone chargers, cash, water, N95 masks
  • Pet carrier, leash, food & records

Plan now

  • Register for county emergency alerts
  • Agree on one out-of-area contact
  • Know two ways out (165, 96, 78, 67 often closed)
  • Keep gas tank above half; back car in
  • Check on neighbors who live alone
